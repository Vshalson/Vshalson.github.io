And in American culture, it's interpreted that to be happy you must follow your passion What cal explains that "follow your passion is bad advice"

Newport's law: Telling a young person to follow their passion reduces the probability they will end up passionate.

  1. Follow your passion presuppose that you have an existing passion that you can identify and follow
  2. It also presupposes that if you really like something and then match that to your job you will then form out sort of long term engaging and meaningful career. Most ironic e.g of the insufficiency of this advice is Steve jobs itself is that he make other people follow your passion but his biographer make it clear that it's not at all that he did "in the period leading up the apple company, Steve Jobs doesn't have an unusual passion for technology, entrepreneurship apple was something in which he stumbled into"--one of his biographers tell that apple was a small-time scheme that unexpectedly took off but of course no one doubts Steve jobs ended up quite passionate about what he did for a living --> lesson here is it's not how you get started that matters maybe What matters is what you do once you get going.
